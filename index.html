Marwin's Finance Notes

Educational notes on business models, financial systems, and long-term thinking

What is included in this website:

This website is a learning archive documenting my journey in understanding businesses, financial institutions, and capital allocation. Rather than focusing on short-term market performance, I study business models, incentives, and durability to improve my judgment over time. Some pieces reflect earlier thinking and experimentation.

Research Coverage:

Fundamental Analysis:

I focus on understanding how businesses actually make money. This includes studying business models, competitive advantages, financial strength, and key risks, with an emphasis on whether a company can protect capital and compound value over the long term.

Technical Analysis:

Technical analysis is used as a supporting tool to observe price behavior, trends, and market sentiment. It helps frame timing and positioning, but it never replaces conviction built from fundamentals.

Industry Outlook:

Each company is analysed within its broader industry context. I look at long-term structural trends, competitive dynamics, and external forces that influence how value is created or destroyed across economic cycles.

Concept Notes:

This section is more personal and reflective. It captures ideas, lessons, and questions that arise from studying businesses, markets, and real-world decision-making. These notes reflect my curiosity about capital discipline, responsibility, and why some systems endure while others fail.

Portfolio & Allocation:

This section provides a transparent view of how capital is allocated across assets and ideas. The focus is not on short-term performance, but on understanding concentration, risk exposure, and how decisions evolve.
